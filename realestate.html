How it all started

Welcome to High School

Real Estate for me started back in high school. Initially, I simply wanted to help my mother out with her own real estate business. She had just started out and I decided to join her on her prospecting. I grew to enjoy walking around and speaking with neighbors about their lives, their current housing situations, and slipping in the occasional pitch.

As my mom got her first couple of leads, I listened in on the process. When "the first couple" turned to dozens, I wanted to be a part of the process myself. I would wake up on the weekends to go strategically place Open House signs on the road. I would help with contract preparation. I did whatever I could to help my mom spend more time with her clients, and less time doing contract prep work and paperwork.

Soon, I gained intrest in the market itself. What determined prices? How did my mom know how much a home would sell before before it even sold. Why was Redfin and Zillow so wrong all the time about sale prices. I Started looking at the available tools and my mother instructed me on how to build reports and analyze the market.

Happy 18th Birthday

So I turned 18. I already knew a fair bit about the space. I certainly wasn't up to anything fancy the summer before college. So, I took up real estate education. I studied day and night for the sales associate exam, taking online classes from Chamberlain Real Estate School.

I finished my education in August, and filled out a request with the California Department of Real Estate to take the exam. In October, my request was processed.

I had a Chemistry 1A lecture that morning, but the exam came first. I hopped in an Uber to the Oakland testing location and took that exam instead of going to class. 1 hour later, I walked out, triumphant. I was officially a licensed realtor. Time to start reaching out to my connections!

The Summer of 2017

My license was proudly filed with Intero. Now came the hardwork. I spent a great deal of time going through the same process as I did in high school. Prospecting, finding open houses to host, working with clients. It was a huge step up from filling out paperwork and doing the market research.

Needless to say, I began to grow my real estate identity. I closed 5 transactions that summer. I learned a great deal about salesmanship, as well as empathy and much more about the inner workings of the real estate process.

The experience had a profound impact on me and served me well in writing one of the essays which led to my acceptance to the Haas School of Business. You can read that essay here.

Licensed in October 2016!
